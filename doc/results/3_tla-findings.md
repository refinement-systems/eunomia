attempt from `doc/plans/0_tla-optimization.md` so the effort leaves a trail

## B4 — `SYMMETRY Permutations(CapIds)` on the safety arm

**Status: adopted — a sound, large state-space quotient.** Extending the safety
arm's symmetry to permute `CapIds` as well as `Procs` collapses each
permutation-orbit of *both* model-value sets. The safety arm drops **7,264,485 →
1,240,344 distinct (a 5.86× further reduction on top of B3's Procs quotient;
9.82× total over the un-quotiented 12,183,480)**, with **every invariant verdict
unchanged**, the **diameter unchanged (28)**, all nine negative controls — incl. a
new *cap-asymmetric* one — still tripping, and a deliberately-injected
cap-asymmetric bug still caught under the quotient. As in B3 this is the one case
where a distinct-state reduction is *not* a coverage regression: a sound quotient
checks the same behaviours in fewer states.

The plan tagged B4 *investigate — highest potential reduction, highest
silent-unsoundness risk*, the risk being the asymmetric `InitCap == CHOOSE c \in
CapIds` seed over the structured `parent` forest. That risk is the same kind B3
cleared for `Procs`/`InitProc`, validated here with the identical adversarial
methodology (exact-factor check + symmetric negative control + injected
asymmetric bug, now on the cap axis). Symmetry is **unsound under a liveness
property** in TLC, so the cap symmetry — like the proc one — lives only on the
invariant-only safety arm, never on the liveness `CapRevocation.cfg`.

Per the project decision recorded in the plan, **`CapIds` stays at 4** here: this
is a same-coverage speed/soundness result (identical reachable set, fewer orbit
representatives). The headroom the quotient unlocks — running the safety arm at
larger `CapIds` for deeper-CDT coverage — is quantified below and left as a
follow-up.

### The change

Pure spec/cfg/tooling; **no action, invariant, or property body changed.**

* **`tla/cap_revocation/CapRevocation.tla`** — a `CapSymmetry ==
  Permutations(CapIds)` operator and a combined
  `SafetySymmetry == ProcSymmetry \cup CapSymmetry` (TLC's idiom for a symmetry
  over two disjoint model-value sets is the union of their `Permutations`, i.e.
  the group generated by both). `ProcSymmetry`/`NotifSymmetry` are unchanged and
  remain in scope. Plus a new cap-asymmetric negative-control bad-spec —
  `AsymCap`/`LeakRevokedCapAsym`/`NextCapAsymBad`/`SpecCapAsymBad` — a third
  sibling of the existing `SpecBad`/`SpecAsymBad` block (see Validation §3).
  Defining an unused operator/spec is inert for every cfg that does not name it,
  so the liveness arm and the other controls are behaviourally untouched.
* **`CapRevocation_Safety.cfg`** — `SYMMETRY ProcSymmetry` → `SYMMETRY
  SafetySymmetry`.
* **`CapRevocation_Safety_NegControl.cfg`** and **`CapRevocation_AsymBug.cfg`** —
  the two committed symmetric guards switch to `SYMMETRY SafetySymmetry` so they
  run under the *exact* symmetry the safety arm now declares.
* **`CapRevocation_CapAsymBug.cfg`** (new) — the CapIds-axis soundness guard:
  `SpecCapAsymBad` at the safety-arm constants under `SYMMETRY SafetySymmetry`,
  asserting `DeadNowhere`. The dual of the existing (proc-axis)
  `CapRevocation_AsymBug.cfg`. Registered in `scripts/tla-neg-controls.sh` (now
  nine controls); the `model` CI job already runs that script.
* **`tools/tla/model-manifest.tsv`** — the `CapRevocation_Safety` row re-pinned
  to the post-quotient distinct count (1,240,344; diameter 28 unchanged), with
  the header comment updated to name `SafetySymmetry` and the new pre/post
  counts.

### Why this is a sound quotient, not lost coverage

`CapIds` is interchangeable in exactly the way `Procs` is: every action and
invariant quantifies over cap ids uniformly and none hard-codes a specific one.
`parent` maps `CapIds` to `CapIds`, so a cap permutation carries a CDT forest to
an isomorphic one — the structured forest is permuted *with* the ids, not broken
by them. The only initial asymmetry is the `InitCap == CHOOSE c \in CapIds` seed
(`Init` gives the root cap to exactly `InitCap`), the structurally-risky
asymmetric-`CHOOSE`-over-a-symmetric-set case the plan flagged. The choice is
itself symmetric (any cap serves equally as the root) and TLC canonicalises the
`CHOOSE` consistently; the validation below confirms the quotient is sound *at
these constants* empirically — adoption was gated on the adversarial controls, not
a passing run.

### Measurements (cold, `-workers 4`, `-fp 0 -fpmem 0.5 -coverage 1`)

| arm (cfg) | distinct | generated | gen:dist | diam | wall (4w) | verdict |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Safety — no symmetry (pre-quotient) | 12,183,480 | 138,167,803 | 11.3 | 28 | 6m26s | No error |
| Safety — `ProcSymmetry` (B3, before) | 7,264,485 | 76,871,188 | 10.6 | 28 | 3m56s | No error |
| **Safety — `SafetySymmetry` (after)** | **1,240,344** | **13,194,241** | **10.6** | **28** | **1m18s** | **No error** |

* **Marginal CapIds reduction 5.86×** on top of the Procs quotient (7,264,485 →
  1,240,344); **generated 5.83×** (76.9M → 13.2M).
* **Total quotient 9.82×** over the un-quotiented set (12,183,480 → 1,240,344);
  **generated 10.47×** (138.2M → 13.2M). The factors compose: B3's Procs 1.677× ×
  this 5.86× ≈ 9.82×.
* **Diameter unchanged** (28): the quotient reaches every orbit-representative at
  the same BFS depth — the structural-equivalence check.

### Why the total factor is 9.82×, not 48× (honest accounting)

The combined symmetry group has order `|Procs|! × |CapIds|! = 2 × 24 = 48`, so a
perfectly free action would give 48×. By Burnside the orbit count equals the
average fixed-point count over the group: `orbits = (1/48) Σ_g |Fix(g)|`. With
`orbits = 1,240,344` and the identity fixing all `T = 12,183,480`, the 47
non-identity permutations collectively fix `48 × 1,240,344 − 12,183,480 =
47,353,032` states — an average of **~1.01M each (~8.3% of `T`)**. That large
fixed-point mass is why the realized factor sits at 9.82×, not 48×, and is
structural, not a defect: the model rarely has all four caps live at once (a
state with one or two live caps is fixed by every cap permutation that only moves
the *absent* caps), and B2's `Threads=2`/`QueueDepth=2` enlarges the symmetric
region further — the same measured interaction B3 recorded for `Procs` (its factor
came in at 1.677×, below the size-2 ideal of 2×, for the same reason). The
quotient is still sound and still removes ~10.9M redundant states relative to the
un-quotiented run (~6.0M relative to B3's Procs-only quotient).

### Validation — B3's mandatory guards (all met)

1. **Verdicts + structure unchanged.** The safety arm under `SafetySymmetry`
   reports "No error has been found": all six safety invariants
   (`TypeOK`/`MoveSemantics`/`DeadNowhere`/`LiveParent`/`FireSafe`/`RevokedDead`)
   + `ReportMonotone` hold, and the **diameter is 28** — identical to both
   before-arms. A wrong quotient would move the diameter or flip a verdict;
   neither happened. SANY parses `CapRevocation.tla` clean.
2. **Negative controls still trip** (`scripts/tla-neg-controls.sh`) — all nine,
   including the new cap-asymmetric guard:

```
ok  CapRevocation_NegControl.cfg        LiveParent violated as expected (exit 12)
ok  CapRevocation_Safety_NegControl.cfg LiveParent violated as expected (exit 12)   # symmetric guard, SafetySymmetry
ok  CapRevocation_AsymBug.cfg           DeadNowhere violated as expected (exit 12)   # proc-asym guard, SafetySymmetry
ok  CapRevocation_CapAsymBug.cfg        DeadNowhere violated as expected (exit 12)   # cap-asym guard (new), SafetySymmetry
ok  CapRevocation_NegLiveness.cfg       EventuallyRevoked violated as expected (13)
ok  CommitProtocol_NegControl.cfg       RecoverReconstructs violated as expected (13)
ok  IpcReactor_NegControl.cfg           NoLostWakeup violated as expected (12)
ok  IpcReactor_NegBackpressure.cfg      NoLostWakeupWritable violated as expected (12)
ok  IpcReactor_NegLostWakeup.cfg        NoLostWakeup violated as expected (12)
```

   The symmetric `CapRevocation_Safety_NegControl` (`SpecBad`, interior-delete →
   `LiveParent`) still trips under the combined symmetry — proof the quotient does
   not prune a bug present in every orbit member. But a symmetric bug survives
   even a *wrong* quotient, so it cannot probe over-breadth; that is the next
   guard's job.
3. **Injected cap-asymmetric-bug probe** (the direct test of the `InitCap CHOOSE`
   risk — the new control). `LeakRevokedCapAsym` leaks the *specific* dead cap
   `AsymCap` (a fixed non-`InitCap` id) into a cspace — a genuine `DeadNowhere`
   violation that singles out **one cap model value**, so it breaks the
   CapIds-symmetry premise and its path is reachable only after a real
   Copy-then-revoke, traversing states the quotient collapses. Run at the safety
   constants both ways:

   | probe | result |
   |---|---|
   | without symmetry (control: bug is real) | `Invariant DeadNowhere is violated` (313 distinct) |
   | **with `SYMMETRY SafetySymmetry`** | **`Invariant DeadNowhere is violated`** (205 distinct) |

   The combined quotient does **not** hide the cap-asymmetric violation. This is
   the load-bearing CapIds check: where the symmetric guard (§2) and the
   proc-asymmetric `CapRevocation_AsymBug` (which singles out a *process*) cannot
   detect an over-broad *cap* symmetry, this one — singling out a *cap* — does.
   (Mechanism: TLC checks each invariant on the generated orbit-representative
   *before* the seen-test, and `DeadNowhere` is itself symmetric, so a violating
   orbit cannot be silently canonicalised away — confirmed in practice despite the
   asymmetric `CHOOSE` seed.) The smaller distinct count under symmetry (205 vs
   313) is expected: the quotient reaches the violating orbit through fewer
   representatives.
4. **Liveness arm untouched.** `CapRevocation.cfg` (which names no symmetry) is
   unchanged and still pins 503,070 / diameter 22, so `EventuallyRevoked`'s
   verdict is unaffected — the new operators and bad-spec add no obligations to
   any cfg that does not name them.

### Cost / CI-wall-clock judgement

* The safety arm's own run is **~3× faster** (3m56s → 1m18s with coverage; the CI
  `model-safety` job runs without `-coverage`, so proportionally similar). It is a
  **separate parallel job**, so *total* CI wall-clock is unchanged — it remains
  gated by the pre-existing liveness pole, not this arm. The `model-safety` job's
  15-min timeout now has far more margin.
* The `model` job gains one more negative control (the cap-asymmetric guard),
  which finds its counterexample in the first few BFS levels — a few seconds.

### Honest framing

Like B2/B3 this is fundamentally a **coverage / headroom** play, not a
critical-path speedup. The dominant liveness arm (`CapRevocation.cfg`, the
`model` job's pole) is untouched and gains nothing — symmetry is unsound under its
`EventuallyRevoked` tableau. The win is (a) a real but off-critical-path ~3×
speedup of the safety arm, (b) ~6.0M fewer redundant states explored than the
Procs-only quotient (~10.9M fewer than un-quotiented), and (c) the headroom the
plan named B4 *for*: the safety arm can now run at larger `CapIds` (deeper CDT
breadth) inside the same budget. Per the recorded decision, that constant bump is
**not** spent here — `CapIds` stays at 4, banking the speedup and the soundness
result; raising it is a separate, measured coverage change (see Follow-ups).

### Decision

**Adopted.** A sound 5.86× further quotient (9.82× total) with every verdict
intact, the diameter preserved, all nine negative controls — including a new
cap-asymmetric guard — still tripping, and an injected cap-asymmetric bug still
caught under the quotient. The plan tagged B4 *investigate, highest
silent-unsoundness risk*; the measurement and the four guards clear the risk and
support adopting. The largest single reduction the plan identified is realized,
and the soundness concern the asymmetric `InitCap CHOOSE` seed carried is
empirically resolved by the cap-asymmetric probe.

### Follow-ups (out of scope here)

- **Raising `CapIds` on the safety arm** — the headroom this quotient unlocks, and
  the payoff the plan attached to B4. At `CapIds = 4` the safety arm now explores
  1,240,344 states in ~1m18s; the freed budget can instead buy deeper-CDT
  coverage (`CapIds = 5,6,7`). Deferred by decision to keep this attempt a
  same-coverage speed/soundness result with a clean before/after; raising the
  constant changes what is verified (a strict coverage gain) and needs its own
  measured PR — re-pin the manifest to the new larger reachable set and re-check
  the `model-safety` job's 15-min timeout.
- **Threads symmetry** (carried over from B3). `Threads = {t0,t1}` on the safety
  arm is a third size-2 permutable set; `SafetySymmetry \cup
  Permutations(Threads)` would compound further. Deferred to keep to the plan's
  one-change-at-a-time discipline; the operator and the symmetric-/asymmetric-
  negative-control pattern generalise to it directly.

### Note on the throwaway reachability control

The cap-asymmetric probe's *no-symmetry* arm (the §3 "bug is real" row, 313
distinct) was run from a throwaway `CapRevocation_CapAsymBug_NoSym_TMP.cfg` —
`CapRevocation_CapAsymBug.cfg` minus its `SYMMETRY SafetySymmetry` line — created
on the fly and removed after the run, exactly as B3 did for its proc-asymmetric
probe. It is deliberately **not** committed: it proves only that the bug is
reachable, which the standing guard does not need to re-assert on every CI run.
The committed `CapRevocation_CapAsymBug.cfg` covers the symmetric side; the
no-symmetry side is reproducible on demand via `scripts/tla-baseline.sh
--no-symmetry` once the cfg is in the manifest, or by re-stripping the line. Only
the symmetric guard is the soundness monitor, so only it is committed.
